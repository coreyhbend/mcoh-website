Iconic Places

Central Oregon’s most
remarkable places

All destinations
Terrebonne · 30 min from Bend
Smith Rock State Park
Oregon’s crown jewel. Vertical basalt columns, the Crooked River, world-class climbing, and the kind of views that make people reconsider where they live.
Explore Smith Rock
Deschutes National Forest
Mt Bachelor
3,365 acres of terrain. Year-round access. Summit views that stretch to the Willamette Valley. In summer, lift rides, mountain biking, wildflowers.
Plan your visit
Deschutes County
Cascade Lakes Highway
Oregon’s most scenic drive. 66 miles of volcanic peaks, alpine lakes, lava flows, and the kind of sky that reminds you how big the world really is.
Drive the route
Sisters, Oregon
The Town of Sisters
Old West storefronts backed by three Cascade peaks. Sisters Quilt Show draws thousands. Small town done exactly right.
Explore Sisters
Tumalo State Park
Tumalo Falls
A 97-foot waterfall tucked into the Cascade foothills. Three miles from town, but it feels like another world. Perfect for a summer morning before the crowds hit.
See the falls
Bend, Oregon
High Desert Museum
Living raptors, river otters, and rotating exhibitions that make you understand why this landscape is irreplaceable.
Discover the museum
Downtown Bend
Old Mill District
Boutique shops, river access, concerts at Les Schwab Amphitheater, and the best people-watching in Bend.
Visit the Old Mill
Warm Springs · 1 hr north
Kah-Nee-Ta & Warm Springs
Resort, hot springs, and cultural destination on the Warm Springs Reservation. One of the only high-desert hot spring resorts in the Pacific Northwest.
Explore Kah-Nee-Ta
Crook County · 45 min east
Ochoco National Forest
Ponderosa pines, wildflower meadows, thunderegg hunting, and trails that never get crowded. One of the best-kept secrets in Oregon.
Discover the Ochocos
Jefferson County · 45 min north
Lake Billy Chinook
A reservoir carved between canyon walls where three rivers meet. Houseboating, fishing, cliff jumping, and summer days that are impossible to replicate anywhere else in Oregon.
Plan your lake day
Deschutes County · 30 min south
Newberry Volcanic Monument
Obsidian flows, Paulina Lake, East Lake hot springs, and Paulina Peak at 7,984 feet. La Pine’s front yard. Most people don’t know it exists.
Explore Newberry
Insider Picks

What the locals
actually love

This isn’t a list pulled from Yelp or a travel blog written by someone passing through. These are the spots that people who live here keep coming back to.

01
Crux Fermentation Project at sunset
The patio faces west. Order the Tough Love IPA, wait for the light to hit the Cascades, and try not to start looking at real estate listings.
Brewery
02
Smith Rock via Misery Ridge at first light
Arrive at 6:30am. The parking lot is empty, the light is orange, and the wall of rock makes you understand why photographers drive here from three states away.
Trails
03
Sparks Lake at any time of year
Summer: kayaks, wildflowers, South Sister reflection. Winter: snowshoe access, complete silence, and the feeling that you’ve found something most people miss entirely.
Lakes
04
Drake Park on a Thursday farmers market day
Local vendors, Mirror Pond, coffee from one of three roasters, and the most pleasant version of Bend’s community vibe compressed into two hours.

Full community guides

Central Oregon isn’t just Bend. The region spans dozens of distinct neighborhoods and towns, each with a different pace, price point, and lifestyle. Start here.

Awbrey Butte

Elevated, panoramic, established. Views of the Cascades and Bend below. Mature trees, custom homes, and direct access to the Deschutes River Trail system.

Midtown Bend
Bend · Central
Walkable to breweries, restaurants, and the river. Mix of remodeled bungalows and newer infill. The most urban Bend gets — which is still very Oregon.
UrbanWalkableRestaurants

Established west-side Bend along the Deschutes corridor. Mix of mid-century homes and newer builds. Strong rental demand and central location.

Redmond
City · 15 min north of Bend
Bend’s fastest-growing neighbor. Lower prices, more land, and a downtown that’s genuinely finding its stride. Roberts Field Airport access and a community identity that’s entirely its own.

Tumalo
Unincorporated · 10 min NW of Bend
Rural feel, Deschutes River access, and larger parcels with an easy commute to Bend. Agricultural roots with modern homestead energy. Acreage buyers come here first.
RuralAcreageRiver access

Unincorporated · 20 min north
Gateway community to Smith Rock State Park. Small-town character, agricultural land, and one of the most dramatic backyards in the state.
Smith RockSmall townAffordable
Powell Butte
Unincorporated · 20 min SE of Redmond
Rural high desert community between Redmond and Prineville. Large parcels, sweeping open sky, and the kind of space that’s genuinely hard to find closer to Bend.
Very ruralFarmlandSpace
Sisters
City · 25 min west of Bend
Three Fingered Jack, North Sister, South Sister — all visible from main street. Boutique town character, mountain access, arts culture, and a summer event calendar that draws crowds statewide.
Mountain viewsArtsCharming
Sunriver
Resort · 15 min south of Bend
Purpose-built resort community with 35 miles of paved paths, nature center, pools, and a lodge. Strong vacation rental income. A market that performs year-round.
ResortVacation rentalFamily
La Pine
City · 30 min south of Bend
Oregon’s newest city. Larger lots, lower prices, and Newberry National Volcanic Monument as a backyard. Deschutes River frontage and Paulina Lake day trips.
AffordableLarge lotsNewberry
Camp Sherman
Unincorporated · 45 min NW of Bend
A handful of cabins, a general store, and the spring-fed Metolius River — one of the most pristine rivers in the Pacific Northwest. Fly fishing country. Remote and genuinely special.
RemoteFly fishingMetolius River
Prineville

Central Oregon’s original pioneer town. Ochoco National Forest, Prineville Reservoir, and more affordability than anywhere in Deschutes County.

Jefferson County seat and Oregon’s solar eclipse capital. Lake Billy Chinook access, Smith Rock within reach, and one of the best views of Mt Jefferson in the state.
