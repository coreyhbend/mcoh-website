Iconic Places

Central Oregon's most
remarkable places

All destinations
Terrebonne · 30 min from Bend
Smith Rock State Park
Oregon's crown jewel. Vertical basalt columns, the Crooked River, world-class climbing, and the kind of views that make people reconsider where they live.
Explore Smith Rock
Deschutes National Forest
Mt Bachelor
3,365 acres of terrain. Year-round access. Summit views that stretch to the Willamette Valley. In summer, the mountain transforms — lift rides, mountain biking, wildflowers.
Plan your visit
Deschutes County
Cascade Lakes Highway
Oregon's most scenic drive. 66 miles of volcanic peaks, alpine lakes, lava flows, and the kind of sky that reminds you how big the world really is.
Drive the route
Sisters, Oregon
The Town of Sisters
Old West storefronts backed by three Cascade peaks. Sisters Quilt Show draws thousands. The folk festival fills the meadows. Small town done exactly right.
Explore Sisters
Tumalo State Park
Tumalo Falls
A 97-foot waterfall tucked into the Cascade foothills. Three miles from town, but it feels like another world. Perfect for a summer morning before the crowds hit.
See the falls
Bend, Oregon
High Desert Museum
Not a dusty natural history museum. Living raptors, river otters, and rotating exhibitions that genuinely make you understand why this landscape is irreplaceable.
Discover the museum
Downtown Bend
Old Mill District
Boutique shops, river access, concerts at Les Schwab Amphitheater, and the best people-watching in Bend. Former sawmill site turned into something the original owners couldn't have imagined.
Visit the Old Mill
Warm Springs Reservation · 1 hr north
Kah-Nee-Ta & Warm Springs
Resort, hot springs, and cultural destination on the Warm Springs Reservation. One of the only high-desert hot spring resorts in the Pacific Northwest — and one of the most undervisited destinations in Central Oregon.
Explore Kah-Nee-Ta
Crook County · 45 min east
Ochoco National Forest
Ponderosa pines, wildflower meadows, thunderegg hunting, and trails that never get crowded. Prineville's backyard is one of the best-kept secrets in Oregon — and local guides know every mile of it.
Discover the Ochocos
Jefferson County · 45 min north
Lake Billy Chinook
A reservoir carved between canyon walls where three rivers meet — the Deschutes, Crooked, and Metolius. Houseboating, fishing, cliff jumping, and the kind of hot-summer day that's impossible to repeat anywhere else in Oregon.
Plan your lake day
Deschutes County · 30 min south
Newberry National Volcanic Monument
Obsidian flows, Paulina Lake, East Lake hot springs, and the summit of Paulina Peak — 7,984 feet of volcano with views that stretch to Mt Hood. La Pine's front yard. Most people don't know it exists.
Explore Newberry
Insider Picks

What the locals
actually love

This isn't a list pulled from Yelp or a travel blog written by someone passing through. These are the spots that people who live here keep coming back to.

01
Crux Fermentation Project at sunset
The patio faces west. Order the Tough Love IPA, wait for the light to hit the Cascades, and try not to start looking at real estate listings.
Brewery
02
Smith Rock via Misery Ridge at first light
Arrive at 6:30am. The parking lot is empty, the light is orange, and the wall of rock coming out of the fog makes you understand why photographers drive here from three states away.
Trails
03
Sparks Lake at any time of year
Summer: kayaks, wildflowers, South Sister reflection. Winter: snowshoe access, complete silence, and the feeling that you've found something most people miss entirely.
Lakes
04
Drake Park on a Thursday farmers market day
Local vendors, Mirror Pond, coffee from one of three roasters, and the most pleasant version of Bend's community vibe compressed into two hours.

Where You'll Live

Every community has
its own personality

Full community guides

Central Oregon isn't just Bend. The region spans dozens of distinct neighborhoods and towns, each with a different pace, price point, and lifestyle. Start here.

Bend Neighborhoods
Greater Bend Area
Mountain & Resort Towns
High Desert & Regional
Northwest Crossing
Bend · Northwest
Walkable grid streets, a neighborhood market, and strong community identity. Families, trail access, and the kind of neighborhood where people actually know each other.
Walkable Families Trail access
Old Farm District
Bend · Southeast
Newer construction, larger lots, and quiet streets. Close to the Badlands Wilderness. Popular with families who want space without leaving Bend's city limits.
New builds Spacious Quiet
Awbrey Butte
Bend · Northwest
Elevated, panoramic, established. Views of the Cascades and Bend below. Mature trees, custom homes, and direct access to the Deschutes River Trail system.
Views Custom homes Established
Midtown Bend
Bend · Central
Walkable to breweries, restaurants, and the river. Mix of remodeled bungalows and newer infill. The most urban Bend gets — which is still very Oregon.
Urban Walkable Restaurants
River West
Bend · West Side
Established west-side Bend neighborhoods along the Deschutes corridor. Mix of mid-century homes and newer builds. Strong rental demand and central location.
River access Central Established
Southeast Bend
Bend · Southeast
One of Bend's fastest-growing quadrants. Newer neighborhoods, competitive entry prices relative to the west side, and easy access to the Badlands and east desert trails.
Growing Value New builds
Mountain View
Bend · East Side
The most commuter-accessible part of Bend. Established neighborhoods, lower price points than the west side, and a straight shot to Highway 20 and redmond.
Accessible Value Established
Tetherow
Bend · Southwest
Resort community built around a David McLay Kidd golf course. Custom homes, Cascade views, and a community lodge. Southwest Bend's premium address.
Resort Golf Premium
Redmond
City · 15 min north of Bend
Bend's fastest-growing neighbor. Lower prices, more land, and a downtown that's genuinely finding its stride. Roberts Field Airport access, industrial base, and a community identity that's entirely its own.
Value Growth Airport
Tumalo
Unincorporated · 10 min NW of Bend
Rural feel, Deschutes River access, and larger parcels with an easy commute to Bend. Agricultural roots with modern homestead energy. Acreage buyers come here first.
Rural Acreage River access
Terrebonne
Unincorporated · 20 min north of Bend
Gateway community to Smith Rock State Park. Small-town character, agricultural land, and one of the most dramatic backyards in the state. Housing is still affordable here.
Smith Rock Small town Affordable
Powell Butte
Unincorporated · 20 min SE of Redmond
Rural high desert community between Redmond and Prineville. Large parcels, sweeping open sky, and the kind of space that's genuinely hard to find closer to Bend. Agricultural community character.
Very rural Farmland Space
Alfalfa
Unincorporated · East of Bend
Remote high desert community on Bend's eastern fringe. Large ranchland parcels, Badlands Wilderness adjacency, and the clearest night skies in Deschutes County.
Remote Ranchland Dark skies
Sisters
City · 25 min west of Bend
Three Fingered Jack, North Sister, South Sister — all visible from main street. Boutique town character, mountain access, arts culture, and a summer event calendar that draws crowds from across the state.
Mountain views Arts Charming
Sunriver
Resort Community · 15 min south of Bend
Purpose-built resort community with 35 miles of paved paths, its own nature center, pools, and a lodge. Strong vacation rental income. A market that performs year-round regardless of conditions elsewhere.
Resort Vacation rental Family
La Pine
City · 30 min south of Bend
Oregon's newest city. Larger lots, lower prices, and the Newberry National Volcanic Monument as a backyard. Deschutes River frontage, Paulina Lake day trips, and a community growing into itself.
Affordable Large lots Newberry
Camp Sherman
Unincorporated · 45 min NW of Bend
A handful of cabins, a general store, and the spring-fed Metolius River — one of the most pristine rivers in the Pacific Northwest. Fly fishing country. Remote, intentional, and genuinely special.
Remote Fly fishing Metolius River
Prineville
City · 35 min east of Bend · Crook County
Central Oregon's original pioneer town. Ochoco National Forest, thunderegg capital of the world, Prineville Reservoir, and a tight-knit community with more affordability than anywhere in Deschutes County. Home to major tech data centers and quiet growth.
Affordable Ochoco access Growing
Madras
City · 45 min north of Bend · Jefferson County
Jefferson County seat and Oregon's solar eclipse capital — twice. Lake Billy Chinook access, Smith Rock within reach, and one of the best views of Mt Jefferson in the state. Agricultural community with real affordability and room to grow.
Affordable Lake Billy Chinook Agricultural
Warm Springs
Tribal Nation · 1 hr north of Bend
Home of the Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs. Kah-Nee-Ta Resort and Spa, Indian Head Casino, and deep cultural heritage. A significant destination in the regional experience — not just a pass-through on Highway 26.
Kah-Nee-Ta Cultural Hot springs
Culver
City · 30 min north · Jefferson County
Small agricultural community between Redmond and Madras. Lake Billy Chinook access, sweeping Cascade views, and the kind of quiet that has become genuinely rare in the region.
Small town Cascade views Quiet
Maupin
City · 1 hr north · Wasco County
White-water rafting capital of Oregon. The Deschutes River below Pelton Dam is world-class — and Maupin is the staging ground. Tiny town, big recreation identity, and a natural extension of what makes this whole region worth knowing.
Rafting Deschutes River Outdoor
